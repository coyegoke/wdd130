MAIN SALMON TRIPS (RIGGINS AREA)

TRIP DESCRIPTION
The Zambezi This trip focused on the lower gorge, tackling class IV and V rapids. The sheer volume of water is incredible. We navigated 'Oblivion,' a long, powerful rapid with massive standing waves, and 'The Mother,' which demands precise boat placement to avoid the notorious hole. The team's paddling had to be perfectly synchronized. This river requires constant vigilance and respect. The heat, the humidity, and the raw power of the river create an intense, unforgettable experience. It's not just rafting; it's a test of skill and endurance.
The Grand Canyon This expedition through the Grand Canyon is a different kind of rafting. It's about endurance and managing a loaded raft through a variety of rapids, from the powerful 'Hance Rapid' to the technical Granite Rapid. The river's character changes constantly, from calm stretches with stunning scenery to intense whitewater. The logistics of a multi-day trip are demanding, with camping on river beaches and managing supplies. The geological history visible in the canyon walls adds another layer to the experience. It's a journey through time as much as a whitewater adventure.
The Futaleufú This is a whitewater paradise. The crystal-clear water and the surrounding Andes Mountains create a breathtaking backdrop. This trip focused on the upper section, with its continuous class IV and V rapids. 'Inferno Canyon' lived up to its name, demanding aggressive paddling and precise maneuvers. The 'Bridge to Bridge' section is a relentless series of challenging rapids. This river requires a high level of technical skill and teamwork. The water's cold, and the rapids are powerful, but the beauty of the environment makes it worth every paddle stroke.
